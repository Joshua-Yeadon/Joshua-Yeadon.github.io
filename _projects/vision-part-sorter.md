## Requirement

Feed identical parts, scan them, detect faults, reject selected components, and show useful system feedback. The demonstrator had to work and be understandable.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/images/project-details/sorter-prototype.jpg" alt="Annotated industrial part sorter prototype showing part feed, scanning, and sorting path">
  <figcaption>The prototype communicated the intended process clearly: feed, scan, then sort.</figcaption>
</figure>

## Review

The review looked at performance against detection, rejection, feeding, feedback, rapid prototyping, assembly, and testability.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/images/project-details/sorter-issue.jpg" alt="Annotated part sorter mechanism showing access and mounting issues">
  <figcaption>Several issues were not about whether the mechanism moved, but whether it could be assembled and tested cleanly.</figcaption>
</figure>

## Redesign

- Improve mount geometry and bolt access.
- Make subassemblies independently testable.
- Keep the scanning enclosure useful without hiding the demonstration.
- Reduce bespoke awkward parts where rapid-prototyped geometry was enough.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/images/project-details/sorter-redesign.jpg" alt="Sketch redesign of industrial part sorter with annotations for mechanism and assembly improvements">
  <figcaption>The redesign sketch is the useful output: fewer assembly traps, clearer function, easier testing.</figcaption>
</figure>
