## Requirement

Test small pico-hydro generators around the 500 W range. The rig needed usable head, measurable water power, and enough access to install candidate hardware without fighting the machine.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/images/project-details/pico-architecture.jpg" alt="Early sketches comparing pico-hydro test machine layouts and pipe access">
  <figcaption>Architecture sketching focused on access, water storage, pipe losses, and candidate installation.</figcaption>
</figure>

## System

Supply tank, collection tank, pipework, flow and pressure sensing, candidate interface, and electrical logging. The machine supplies water power; the candidate generator proves efficiency.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/images/project-details/pico-calculation.jpg" alt="Moody diagram used for pico-hydro pipe loss and friction factor calculation">
  <figcaption>Pipe losses and flow regime informed the system architecture instead of being left as afterthoughts.</figcaption>
</figure>

## Output

- Product design specification from performance needs.
- Layout comparison around access and water storage.
- Pipe and sensor choices informed by hydraulic calculations.
- Custom flange/interface for the test candidate.
